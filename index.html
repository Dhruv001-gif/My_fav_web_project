My fav websites

1.GeeksforGreeks

GeeksforGeeks (often abbreviated as GFG) is a leading digital repository and learning platform tailored specifically for computer science enthusiasts, students, and professionals. 
Founded by Sandeep Jain in 2008, it has evolved from a simple blog into a massive educational ecosystem. The platform is best known for its meticulously structured tutorials on
data structures and algorithms (DSA), which serve as a "holy grail" for students preparing for technical interviews at top-tier tech companies.

Beyond just coding problems, GFG offers a comprehensive suite of resources including:

1.Interview Experiences: Real-world accounts of hiring processes at companies like Google, Amazon, and Microsoft.
2.Practice Portal: An interactive coding environment where users can solve problems categorized by difficulty levels (Basic, Easy, Medium, Hard).
3.Article Contribution: A community-driven model where developers can write and publish technical articles, fostering a culture of knowledge sharing.
4.Specialized Courses: Both free and paid bootcamps covering trending technologies like Machine Learning, Web Development, and Gate preparation.

Because of its clear, "bottom-up" approach to explaining complex logic, it has become an indispensable tool for anyone looking to master the foundations of programming or pivot
into a career in software engineering.

2.Wikipedia

Wikipedia is a vast, multilingual online encyclopedia that has redefined how the world accesses and shares information since its launch on January 15, 2001. Founded
by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger, the platform is hosted by the non-profit Wikimedia Foundation and operates on a model of open collaboration. Unlike traditional encyclopedias
written by experts, Wikipedia relies on a global community of volunteer editors who use wiki-based software to write, update, and refine millions of articles in real-time.
This decentralized approach allows it to cover an unparalleled range of topics—from niche pop culture to complex scientific theories—making it the largest and most-read reference
work in human history. To maintain its integrity, the community adheres to strict core pillars, including a neutral point of view, verifiability through reliable citations, and
a firm ban on original research. While it has faced criticism over the years regarding systemic bias and the potential for vandalism, its radical transparency and commitment to
free knowledge have made it a cornerstone of the modern internet and a vital resource for billions of people worldwide.

3.Chatgpt

ChatGPT is an AI chatbot from OpenAI that talks, writes, and solves problems like a human. Since its late 2022 launch, it has become a go-to tool for everything from coding
to creative writing.
1.How it works: It's based on Large Language Models (like GPT-4o) trained on massive amounts of internet text to predict and generate natural responses.
2.What it does: It can summarize docs, write emails, analyze data, and even "see" images or "hear" your voice.
3.The catch: It sometimes "hallucinates" (makes things up), so it’s best to double-check facts.
You can use it for free on the web or via mobile apps, with a "Plus" subscription available for more power.
